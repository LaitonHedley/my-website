About Me

I am a PhD researcher in Psychological Science at the University of Newcastle, studying how people behave, coordinate, and perform together in dynamic, cognitively demanding team environments.

My research combines experimental psychology, cognitive modelling, behavioural time series analysis, and interactive task design to better understand human and machine teamwork.

I am currently seeking postdoctoral positions where I can push the boundaries of psychological research using dynamic tasks, time-series analyses, and rigorous quantifiable metrics to advance the understanding of human psychology.

Research Interests

🧮

Mathematical Psychology

Using computational cognitive models to study human behaviour and cognition, formalising psychological theory into testable, quantitative frameworks.

📈

Time-Series Analysis

Theoretical and practical application of time-series methods to capture how behaviour unfolds and changes dynamically over time.

🔓

Open Science

Developing accessible tools and packages — including contributions to the jamovi software ecosystem — to support transparent, reproducible research.

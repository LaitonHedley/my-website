About Me

I am a PhD researcher in Psychological Science at the University of Newcastle. I study how people coordinate with each other and with intelligent machines in dynamic team environments.

My research combines experimental psychology, cognitive modelling, behavioural time series analysis, and interactive task design to better understand human and machine teamwork.

I am currently seeking postdoctoral positions where I can push the boundaries of human–machine teaming research and contribute to a collaborative, rigorous research environment.

Research Interests

�

Human–Machine Teaming

Investigating how humans and AI systems collaborate in shared dynamic tasks, and what drives effective joint performance.

🧠

Team Coordination

Studying the cognitive and behavioural mechanisms that enable people to coordinate fluidly in multi-agent environments.

📈

Behavioural Time Series

Applying time series methods to capture the temporal structure of coordinated behaviour and joint action dynamics.

🔬

Cognitive Modelling

Building computational models to formalise theories of human performance and predict behaviour in complex team tasks.
